Snowballs

Description

These cookies are made with simple ingredients and taste amazing. The shape makes them sort of resemble snowballs. These are a staple of Mexican weddings and pair great with tea. Usually made during the holdiays which adds to their festive look.

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup of powdered sugar, or more as needed
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugare
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup very finely chopped pecans
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Place 1/2 cup powdered sugar in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Beat butter, remaining 1/2 cup powdered sugar, and vanilla with an electric mixer in a large bowl until smooth. Gradually mix in flour, pecans, and salt utnil completely incorporated. Roll dough into walnut-sized balls and place 2 inches apart onto greased baking sheets.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven until bottoms are light golden brown but tops are still pale, 12 to 15 minutes. (Try not to let cookies get too brown: it's better to undercook them than to overcook them.)
  4. Remove cookies from the oven and let sit on the baking sheets for a moment before removing them from the wire racks.
  5. Roll hot cookies in the reserved powdered sugar to coat, then return to wire racks to cool. Once cooled, roll cookies in the powdered sugar once more.
